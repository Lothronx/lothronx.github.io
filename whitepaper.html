Global South Friendship, Growing Tie

The trading relationship of China with Latin America and the Caribbean (LAC) grew 26-fold between the years 2000 and 2020 and it doesn’t show any signs of decreasing. China-LAC trade is expected to be more than doubled by 2035, with an expected growth of more than $700 billion. China’s Belt and Road initiative (BRI) has encouraged the trading and helped cultivate a trust relationship between Asian companies and their Latin American and Caribbean counterparts.

Imports and Exports, 2022

Latin American exports to China consist primarily of natural resources, including ores (42%), soybeans (16%), mineral fuels and oils (10%), meat (6%), and copper (5%).

China’s major exports to LAC include electrical machinery and equipment (21%), machinery and mechanical appliances (15%), and motor vehicles and parts (7%).

China is the top trading partner of Brazil, Chile, Peru, and Uruguay and the second-largest trading partner for many other LAC countries.
