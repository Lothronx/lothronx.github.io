Frequently Asked Questions

These are the questions we got asked the most often. Here are our answers.

Are there any restrictions for trading between China and Latin America?

Generally it can be said that there aren’t any restrictions for trading between China and South America, but it is better to look for information on any restrictions there might be imposed to your country by any other nation or prohibition on importing/exporting certain products from/to The shipping destination/origin. For example; it is internationally known that merchandise from countries in conflict is always difficult or not allowed to deal with, countries with restrictions from the US have it really difficult to export their products and when they find a buyer is almost impossible to find a shipping company willing to transport the cargo.

What currency is used for payment in both ends?

As currency of global use the USD is always welcomed as payment, but strong currencies like Euros and British Pound are sometimes welcomed too, it all depends on the arrangements you get to, with the buyer/seller, if you have the funds you might even be able to pay in CNY but it all depends on what the part who is receiving the funds is comfortable with.

How to transport your goods from China to Latin America and vice versa?

There are thousands of shipping companies transporting cargo daily from/to China and South America but it all the depends on the price, product, service and way of transport (Air and sea), that you’ll be needing for your cargo. Most of the time the seller ( when importing from China covers the shipping fee, but it may vary for different countries, seller and/or product).

How long does it takes to receive and deliver?

It all depends on how fast the seller arrange the cargo for its shipping after that you’ll have to consider where it has been shipped from if it is from China some times your cargo can get stuck in the port for days if the seller didn’t have all the documents in order but it almost never happens you’ll have to consider the are millions of containers being shipped out of China on a daily basis, if you’re shipping off South America then you’ll know all the things that can and can not happen if you don’t have everything in other and arrange it all with a good shipping company. The total air-cargo transit time between South America and China can vary from 8 to 15 days it all depends which country you’re shipping from and I if it is Ocean freight it could take from six weeks to several more.

Where do they arrive?

You will have to take in consideration the address of the buyer and where do they want to receive the cargo and your capability and/or the shipping agency’s to deliver the cargo to the desired destination, if the buyer will be in charge of the shipping then it will be less trouble for the seller. If you’re the buyer remember to always clearly specify where do you want to receive your goods and to ask the seller or shipping company if they are in willing to deliver to you country. (Don’t forget the restrictions).

How to deal with customs when the shipment arrives?

Of course you will have to have all the documents related to your cargo on hand ready to be given to customs ( sanitary documents, purchasing bills, certificates of origin…) different documents are needed for different cargoes, you will probably have to go through a cargo inspection too. It is always a good Idea to hire a good customs agent if you don’t have the time to deal with this, they could be a little expensive but if they are professionals you will have your goods at your door in no time.

How to not get scammed when buying online from China?

It is important to always check the specifications of the product you are buying you will not want to buy a “ phone” and then get just a “phone case“ , take a look at the reviews of the shop it will give you and Idea of what you’re getting into.

How to check on a supplier from China?

Do not forget to always ask the seller for its business license there you will find a number and a QR code that you can scan and it will take you to the website of the National Enterprise Credit Information if the website is working well, then you’ll be able to type the company’s name or identification number and you’ll be able to see if they’re in a black list or not if there is not information appearing it is always good to go to the provincial credit administration website type in the name and all the needed info will be shown.
